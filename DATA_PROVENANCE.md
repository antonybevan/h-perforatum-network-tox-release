# Data Provenance & Reproducibility

## Authoritative inputs
The committed processed artefacts under `data/processed/` (networks, targets, DILI
sets, liver proteome) are the **authoritative inputs** for all analyses. Their
SHA-256 checksums are recorded in `data/CHECKSUMS.sha256` (verify with
`sha256sum -c data/CHECKSUMS.sha256`).

## External sources and how they were obtained
- **STRING v12.0** functional association network (combined score; evidence channels:
  experiments, databases, co-expression, neighbourhood, fusion, co-occurrence, text
  mining). Raw `string_links.txt.gz` / `string_info.txt.gz` are not committed (size);
  the committed `network_{700,900}.parquet` are the authoritative derivatives.
  Note: STRING combined score is a **functional association**, not a purely physical
  binding network.
- **GTEx v8** gene median TPM (`data/raw/GTEx_*.gct`, committed) — liver column,
  TPM >= 1 filter.
- **DisGeNET** curated gene-disease associations (`data/raw/curated_gene_disease_associations.tsv`,
  committed) — UMLS C0860207 (Drug-Induced Liver Injury). The resulting DILI set is a
  **curated association module, not a validated causal gene set**.
- **FDA DILIrank 2.0** (`data/external/DILIrank_2.0.xlsx`, committed) — chemical-similarity
  control only.
- **Hyperforin targets:** literature-curated (see Supplementary target-evidence table).
- **Quercetin targets:** ChEMBL bioactivity-derived (CHEMBL159; human, IC50/Ki/EC50 <= 10 uM).
  The programmatic retrieval is documented here and in `DATA_MANIFEST.md`; `targets_raw.csv`
  is the committed snapshot used by the pipeline.

## Environment
The committed results were produced under the pinned environment in
`requirements-lock.txt` (Python 3.12.13; see `reproducibility.lock.yml` for the full
manifest). The non-pinned `requirements.txt` gives lower bounds; use
`requirements-lock.txt` for exact reproduction. R figure scripts require the packages
listed in `reproducibility.lock.yml` (r section).

## Why processed files are authoritative
The committed `network_700.parquet` (columns gene1/gene2) and `network_900.parquet`
(columns protein1/protein2/weight, gene-symbol values) were generated by an earlier
extraction pass than the current `scripts/extract_string_network.py` (which emits
gene1/gene2/score); they are therefore not byte-reproducible from that script without
the original STRING raw files. They are retained as the authoritative inputs and
checksummed; re-extraction from STRING raw would change only edge-attribute columns,
not the node/edge sets used downstream.

## STRING text-mining sensitivity (literature-circularity robustness)
Because the DILI module is DisGeNET-derived (literature-based) and the STRING combined
score includes a text-mining channel, some target--DILI edges could reflect literature
co-mention rather than independent evidence. Following Huang et al., *Cell Systems* 2018
(doi:10.1016/j.cels.2018.03.001), `scripts/run_string_textmining_sensitivity.py` rebuilds
the interactome after removing edges supported **solely** by the STRING text-mining channel
(every non-text-mining channel zero) and re-runs all three proximity metrics.

Inputs (raw, gitignored under `data/external/` since they are not committed; SHA-256
recorded here for verification rather than in `CHECKSUMS.sha256`, which only lists
committed artefacts):
- `9606.protein.links.detailed.v12.0.txt.gz` — STRING v12.0 human per-channel scores
  (stringdb-downloads.org, `protein.links.detailed.v12.0`, taxon 9606).
  SHA-256 `28a2ea3b22c964e691c0f2f934c5e3e397a4b0a12c833d8787fa02b7847b91b6`.
- `string_gene_map.txt.gz` — STRING v12.0 ENSP -> preferred gene-symbol map.
  SHA-256 `851493473b0e9dc586a1397525442c8e8c5d31ddf0e15d3148a10c3fd08a5639`.

Integrity gate: rebuilding the network from the per-channel file **without** the text-mining
filter reproduces 100% of the committed `network_{700,900}.parquet` edges (0 missing; a
<0.5% superset arises because `string_gene_map.txt` maps a few more proteins than the
original `string_info` file, and is immaterial — full-rebuild observed values match the
committed values, e.g. d_c Hyperforin 1.30/1.30, RWR 0.1138/0.1137 at >=900). The
full-vs-no-text-mining comparison is therefore internally mapping-consistent. Text-mining-only
edges are 0.7% (>=900) to 2.4% (>=700) of the liver-expressed LCC on which the metrics are
computed (1.4-4.4% of the raw gene network); their removal preserves the Hyperforin>Quercetin
ordering on all three metrics at both thresholds (all p<0.002). Output:
`results/tables/string_textmining_sensitivity.csv`.
